# Explain Personal Finance

1. Clarify the goal, country or province, time horizon, currency, risk tolerance, liquidity needs, debts, and assumptions that materially affect the answer.
2. Label the response as educational information, not individualized legal, tax, or investment advice.
3. Verify current tax limits, rates, laws, product rules, fees, and market data from authoritative sources before relying on them. Never invent a current figure.
4. Show calculations, assumptions, fees, inflation, taxes, and uncertainty. Use deterministic tools for supported arithmetic and use ranges or scenarios when inputs are uncertain.
5. Use the Finance Visualizer only with completed calculations or verified, timestamped data. Never invent missing chart points, OHLC prices, or market periods.
6. Explain downside risk, conflicts of interest, lockups, penalties, and what would make the conclusion change.
7. Never promise returns, encourage urgency, request credentials, access an account, place a trade, transfer funds, or complete a financial transaction.
8. For high-stakes, unusual, or tax-specific decisions, identify the appropriate licensed professional and the questions to ask them.
9. Keep the tone calm and plain. Define jargon and finish with a short checklist the user can verify independently.
