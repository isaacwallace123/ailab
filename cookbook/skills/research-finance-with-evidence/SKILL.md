# Research Finance With Evidence

1. State the exact question, jurisdiction, currency, instrument, and requested as-of time. Distinguish a current quote from historical data, a filing, an estimate, and commentary.
2. Start discovery with Finance Web Research through private SearXNG. Open selected results through the controlled fetcher; extract public filings and reports through the controlled document path. Use official-data tools for central-bank series and regulatory facts.
3. Prefer primary sources: regulators and filings, central banks and statistical agencies, exchanges or licensed feeds, issuer investor-relations pages, then established reporting. Do not treat an aggregator or search snippet as the sole authority.
4. For material current claims, consult at least three useful sources when available. Include at least one primary source and seek a second independent source; do not inflate the count with copied articles.
5. Record each source's title, publisher, URL, publication or observation date, retrieval time, and what claim it supports. Separate event time from article publication time.
6. Reconcile disagreement explicitly. Check currency, exchange, quoted page timestamp, stated delay, adjusted versus unadjusted prices, fiscal periods, restatements, and whether figures are GAAP, IFRS, or non-GAAP.
7. Never treat a search result or snippet as a quote. Never invent a missing value or imply that search-derived, delayed, indicative, or stale data is consolidated real-time data.
8. Finish with a concise evidence table, unresolved conflicts, freshness limitations, and what new evidence would change the conclusion.
