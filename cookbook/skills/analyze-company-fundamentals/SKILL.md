# Analyze Company Fundamentals

1. Confirm the legal issuer, ticker, exchange, jurisdiction, reporting currency, fiscal year-end, and analysis period. Map US issuers to a verified CIK.
2. Start from primary filings and structured regulator data. Capture accession number, form, filing date, fiscal period, units, taxonomy concept, and whether a filing was amended.
3. Build a minimum trend set when available: revenue, operating income, net income, diluted shares and EPS, operating cash flow, capital expenditure, cash, debt, and shareholder equity.
4. Inspect the filing narrative for segment changes, customer concentration, debt maturities, liquidity, contingencies, dilution, related parties, auditor language, and management-defined non-GAAP measures.
5. Do not compare similarly named XBRL concepts blindly. Verify units, duration versus instant facts, consolidated scope, fiscal frames, restatements, and issuer-specific extensions.
6. Separate reported facts, calculations, management claims, analyst estimates, and your inferences. Cite the source beside each material figure.
7. If estimating valuation, state the price timestamp, share-count definition, net-debt inputs, forecast source, discount rate, terminal assumptions, and a sensitivity range. Do not turn a valuation range into a guaranteed target.
8. End with strengths, risks, data-quality limitations, open questions, and conditions that would invalidate the analysis.
