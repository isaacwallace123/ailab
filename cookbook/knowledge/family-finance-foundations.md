# Family Finance Foundations

This collection is educational and is not legal, tax, accounting, or individualized investment
advice. Current contribution limits, tax rules, rates, product terms, and prices must be verified
from authoritative Canadian federal or provincial sources before use.

## Decision order

1. Define the goal, time horizon, currency, and required liquidity.
2. Establish a realistic cash-flow baseline and emergency reserve.
3. Address high-interest debt and confirm employer matching opportunities.
4. Compare registered and non-registered accounts using current rules and personal tax context.
5. Choose diversified, understandable products with transparent fees and risk.
6. Review insurance, beneficiaries, estate documents, and tax implications with qualified
   professionals when appropriate.

## Calculation rules

Show every important assumption: starting amount, periodic contribution, return range, fees,
inflation, tax treatment, compounding frequency, and time horizon. Present scenarios rather than a
single guaranteed outcome. Past performance never guarantees future returns.

## Safety rules

Never request passwords, account numbers, recovery codes, or trading access. Never place a trade,
transfer funds, guarantee returns, or use urgency as persuasion. Explain conflicts of interest,
penalties, lockups, downside risk, and what facts would change a recommendation. For tax filings,
estate planning, complex insurance, or large irreversible decisions, prepare questions for a
licensed Canadian professional.
